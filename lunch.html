Kinilaw na Isda

Freshly caught fish marinated in vinegar, calamansi, ginger, onions, and chili. Siargao’s version often uses tangigue (Spanish mackerel) and coconut vinegar.

Kinilaw na Isda Recipe

Ingredients:

  • Fresh tangigue (Spanish mackerel), cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (coconut vinegar preferred)
  • 2-3 calamansi or 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-inch ginger, julienned
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chilies, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine fish, vinegar, and calamansi/lemon juice in a bowl. Let sit for 10-15 minutes to marinate.
  2. Mix in onions, ginger, and chilies. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Serve immediately or chill before serving for extra flavor.
Adobong Pusit (Squid Adobo)

Tender squid cooked in its ink with garlic, vinegar, and soy sauce. Popular by the beach!

Adobong Pusit Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add squid and cook for 2-3 minutes until tender.
  3. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar, and add bay leaf. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Serve with steamed rice.
Seafood Kare-Kare

A local twist on the peanut stew using crab, shrimp, and squid instead of oxtail.

Seafood Kare-Kare Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 crab, cleaned
  • 200g shrimp, peeled
  • 200g squid, cleaned and sliced
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup water or seafood broth
  • 1/2 cup bagoong (fermented shrimp paste)
  • 1/2 cup banana heart, sliced
  • 1/2 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1/2 cup string beans, cut into pieces

Instructions:

  1. Boil seafood (crab, shrimp, squid) in water or seafood broth for 5-7 minutes until cooked. Set aside.
  2. In a separate pot, sauté peanut butter and mix with the broth to create the kare-kare sauce.
  3. Add vegetables (banana heart, eggplant, string beans) to the sauce and cook until tender.
  4. Return the seafood to the pot and mix. Serve with bagoong on the side.
Inihaw na Panga ng Tuna

Grilled tuna jaw, smoky and juicy, served with soy-vinegar dip and rice.

Inihaw na Panga ng Tuna Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 tuna jaw (panga ng tuna)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp calamansi juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the tuna jaw with soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, calamansi juice, salt, and pepper for 30 minutes.
  2. Grill the tuna jaw over medium heat until smoky and cooked through.
  3. Serve with soy-vinegar dip and steamed rice.
Ginataang Alimasag (Crabs in Coconut Milk)

Mud crabs simmered in creamy coconut milk with leafy greens and chili – rich and flavorful.

Ginataang Alimasag Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg mud crabs, cleaned
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1-inch ginger, sliced
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chilies, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach) or any leafy greens
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, sauté onion and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the crabs and cook for 5-7 minutes, then pour in coconut milk.
  3. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, then add the kangkong and chilies.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, then serve hot.
Paksiw na Isda

A vinegar-based fish stew with eggplant, bitter gourd, and garlic – simple, sour, and savory.

Paksiw na Isda Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg fish (bangus or tilapia), cut into pieces
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bitter gourd, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, layer the fish with garlic, eggplant, and bitter gourd.
  2. Pour in vinegar, water, and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Simmer for 15-20 minutes until the fish is tender and the flavors meld.
  4. Serve hot with steamed rice.
Sinigang na Hipon

Shrimp in tamarind-based sour soup with vegetables – a refreshing coastal comfort dish.

Sinigang na Hipon Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pack sinigang mix or 1/4 cup fresh tamarind paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2-3 long green chili peppers
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, then add tomatoes, onion, and tamarind mix/paste.
  2. Simmer for 5-10 minutes, then add shrimp and cook for another 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add vegetables (eggplant, kangkong) and green chilies, then season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve hot with steamed rice.
Inasal na Manok (Grilled Chicken)

Visayan-style marinated chicken grilled to perfection, often served with spicy soy-vinegar sauce.

Inasal na Manok Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into serving parts
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice
  • 1 tablespoon annatto oil (or regular cooking oil)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, sugar, calamansi juice, and annatto oil to create the marinade.
  2. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  3. Grill the chicken over medium heat for 15-20 minutes or until cooked through, basting with the marinade while grilling.
  4. Serve with spicy soy-vinegar dipping sauce.
Lechon Kawali with Sukang Pinakurat

Crispy pork belly served with local spicy vinegar made in Mindanao – crunchy outside, juicy inside.

Lechon Kawali with Sukang Pinakurat Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • For Sukang Pinakurat: 1/4 cup vinegar, 2-3 bird’s eye chilies, 1/4 cup soy sauce, 1 tbsp brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, boil pork belly with garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt for 1-2 hours until tender.
  2. Let the pork cool and dry completely, then deep fry until crispy.
  3. For the Sukang Pinakurat, mix vinegar, soy sauce, chilies, and brown sugar in a bowl.
  4. Serve the crispy lechon kawali with the spicy vinegar dip on the side.
