Halo-Halo

A refreshing mix of shaved ice, sweetened beans, fruits, leche flan, ube halaya, and evaporated milk. Perfect for cooling down after a beach day.

Halo-Halo Recipe

Ingredients:

  • Shaved ice
  • Sweetened beans (red beans, kidney beans, garbanzos)
  • Sweetened fruits (banana, jackfruit, etc.)
  • Leche flan
  • Ube halaya (purple yam jam)
  • Evaporated milk
  • Pinipig (crispy rice flakes, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Layer the sweetened beans and fruits in a glass or bowl.
  2. Add a scoop of shaved ice on top.
  3. Top with leche flan, ube halaya, and a drizzle of evaporated milk.
  4. Sprinkle with pinipig (optional) and serve with a straw and spoon.
Buko Pandan

Young coconut strips and pandan-flavored gelatin in sweet cream. It’s creamy, aromatic, and very Pinoy.

Buko Pandan Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 young coconut (buko), shredded
  • 1 pack pandan-flavored gelatin, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup all-purpose cream
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk (optional)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the shredded coconut and pandan gelatin cubes.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the condensed milk, all-purpose cream, and coconut milk (optional).
  3. Combine the coconut mixture with the cream mixture and stir well.
  4. Chill for at least 1 hour before serving. Serve chilled in a bowl or glass.
Banana Turon

Caramelized banana (and sometimes jackfruit) wrapped in lumpia wrapper and deep-fried. Crispy outside, gooey inside!

Banana Turon Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe saba bananas, peeled and sliced lengthwise
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup jackfruit (optional)
  • 8-10 lumpia wrappers
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Sprinkle the brown sugar on the sliced bananas, and optionally add jackfruit slices.
  2. Wrap the banana slices (and jackfruit) in lumpia wrappers, folding the sides tightly to seal.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and fry the turon until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Drain excess oil and serve hot. Enjoy the crispy, gooey turon!
Ube Halaya

Purple yam mashed and sweetened into a rich, creamy dessert. Often topped with cheese or coconut cream.

Ube Halaya Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated ube (purple yam)
  • 1 can condensed milk
  • 1 can evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup butter or margarine
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Grated cheese or coconut cream for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat butter in a pan, then add grated ube and stir continuously.
  2. Add condensed milk, evaporated milk, and sugar. Mix well.
  3. Cook over low heat, stirring until thick and smooth (about 30–40 minutes).
  4. Transfer to a mold, let cool, and top with cheese or coconut cream.
Cassava Cake

Made from grated cassava, coconut milk, and condensed milk — soft, dense, and slightly chewy.

Cassava Cake Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated cassava
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix cassava, coconut milk, condensed milk, and sugar until well blended.
  3. Pour into a greased baking dish and bake for 45–60 minutes or until firm.
  4. Top with grated cheese and return to oven for 10 more minutes.
  5. Cool before slicing. Serve warm or chilled.
Mango Float (Mango Royale)

Layers of graham crackers, sweet mangoes, and cream. Chilled to perfection and loved by locals and tourists alike.

Mango Float Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, sliced thinly
  • 1 pack graham crackers
  • 1 cup all-purpose cream
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix all-purpose cream and condensed milk. Chill for 10 minutes.
  2. In a dish, layer graham crackers, then cream mixture, then mango slices.
  3. Repeat the layers until ingredients are used up.
  4. Top with more mangoes or crushed grahams. Chill for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  5. Serve cold and enjoy the creamy, fruity layers!
Buko Pie

A flaky pie crust filled with soft young coconut meat and creamy filling. A Filipino bakery staple!

Buko Pie Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups young coconut meat (buko)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch dissolved in 1 cup coconut water
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 2 unbaked pie crusts (top and bottom)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook coconut meat, sugar, condensed milk, and cornstarch mix until thick.
  3. Pour filling into bottom pie crust. Cover with top crust and seal edges.
  4. Poke holes on top to release steam. Bake for 30–40 minutes until golden.
  5. Cool before slicing and serving.
Coconut Ice Cream

Often homemade in beach cafés, this ice cream highlights Siargao’s signature ingredient: fresh coconut!

Coconut Ice Cream Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup grated fresh coconut (optional)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix all ingredients until sugar is dissolved.
  2. Churn in an ice cream maker, or freeze and stir every 30 minutes for 3 hours.
  3. Let set overnight for best consistency. Serve chilled with toasted coconut flakes.
Camote Cue

Deep-fried caramelized sweet potatoes on skewers — a street-style treat that doubles as dessert or snack.

Camote Cue Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, sliced into rounds
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 cups cooking oil
  • Skewers for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a deep pan. Add sweet potatoes and cook halfway.
  2. Sprinkle in brown sugar and stir until sweet potatoes are fully coated and caramelized.
  3. Remove and drain on paper towels. Skewer for serving.
  4. Best served hot and crispy!
