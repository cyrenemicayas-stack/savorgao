Sinuglaw

A combo of kinilaw (raw marinated fish) and grilled pork — smoky, tangy, and refreshing all at once.

Sinuglaw Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb fresh tuna, cubed
  • 1/2 lb grilled pork belly, diced
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, minced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2–3 chili peppers, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cucumber, diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate tuna in vinegar, ginger, onions, and chili for 10–15 minutes.
  2. Add grilled pork and cucumber. Mix gently.
  3. Season with salt and pepper. Chill before serving.
Pangat na Isda sa Gata

Fish simmered in coconut milk with leafy greens and chili. Creamy and comforting — a Siargao coastal classic.

Pangat na Isda sa Gata Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fish (tilapia, bangus, or mackerel)
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2–3 green chilies (siling haba)
  • 1 bunch spinach or malunggay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, sauté garlic and onion in a bit of oil.
  2. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  3. Add fish and chili. Simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  4. Add leafy greens and cook until wilted.
  5. Season to taste and serve with rice.
Inihaw na Liempo

Marinated pork belly grilled to perfection, often served with garlic rice and spicy vinegar dip.

Inihaw na Liempo Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. Marinate pork in soy sauce, calamansi, garlic, sugar, and pepper for at least 2 hours.
  2. Grill over medium heat until nicely charred and cooked through.
  3. Slice and serve with vinegar dip and garlic rice.
Tinolang Manok

Native chicken cooked in ginger broth with papaya and chili leaves. Light but nourishing — great for cooler Siargao evenings.

Tinolang Manok Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole native chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green papaya, sliced
  • 1 bunch chili leaves (or malunggay)
  • Fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste
  • 6 cups water

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until aromatic.
  2. Add chicken and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in water and bring to a boil, then lower heat to simmer until chicken is tender.
  4. Add papaya and cook until soft.
  5. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper, then add chili leaves. Simmer for 2–3 minutes before serving.
Coconut Curry Prawns

Juicy prawns simmered in a rich coconut curry sauce with local spices — aromatic and deeply flavorful.

Coconut Curry Prawns Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g prawns, cleaned and deveined
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • Salt and chili flakes to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in oil until fragrant.
  2. Add curry powder and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle boil.
  4. Add prawns and bell peppers. Simmer until prawns are pink and cooked.
  5. Season with salt and chili flakes. Serve hot with rice.
Bangus Sisig

A healthier take on traditional sisig using milkfish — crispy, tangy, and spicy, served on a sizzling plate with egg.

Bangus Sisig Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 large bangus (milkfish), deboned and flaked
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 chili, chopped (optional)
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp calamansi juice
  • 1 egg (optional, for sizzling plate)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Fry bangus flakes until slightly crispy. Set aside.
  2. Sauté onion, garlic, and chili until soft.
  3. Mix in bangus flakes, mayonnaise, and calamansi juice.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with optional egg on a sizzling plate.
