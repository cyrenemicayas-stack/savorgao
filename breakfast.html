Tapsilog

A classic Filipino breakfast with marinated beef (tapa), garlic fried rice (sinangag), and fried egg (itlog).

Tapsilog Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kg beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Cooked garlic rice
  • Fried eggs

Instructions:

  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, calamansi, garlic, and sugar for at least 1 hour.
  2. Fry marinated beef until browned and cooked through.
  3. Serve with garlic rice and fried egg.
Dangsilog

Fried dried fish (danggit), garlic rice, and egg – a coastal staple, especially popular in island towns.

Dangsilog Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 pieces danggit (dried fish)
  • Cooked garlic rice
  • Fried eggs
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Fry danggit in hot oil until crispy.
  2. Serve with garlic rice and fried egg.
Coconut Pancakes

Fluffy pancakes made with coconut milk and sometimes topped with grated coconut and tropical fruits.

Coconut Pancakes Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Butter or oil for cooking
  • Optional toppings: grated coconut, tropical fruits, syrup

Instructions:

  1. Mix all ingredients in a bowl to form a batter.
  2. Heat a pan and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  3. Pour batter and cook until bubbles form, flip and cook the other side.
  4. Top with your choice of coconut, fruits, or syrup.
Eggplant Omelette (Tortang Talong)

Grilled eggplant dipped in beaten egg and fried, served with rice and spicy vinegar.

Tortang Talong Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Grill eggplants until soft, then peel the skin and flatten.
  2. Dip flattened eggplant in beaten egg, season with salt and pepper.
  3. Fry until golden brown on both sides.
  4. Serve with rice and spicy vinegar.
Lugaw or Arroz Caldo

Savory rice porridge made with chicken or fish, ginger, and sometimes topped with boiled egg and fried garlic.

Lugaw or Arroz Caldo Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 250g chicken or fish
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 4 cups water or broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: boiled egg, fried garlic, scallions, calamansi

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion and ginger, then add chicken/fish until cooked.
  2. Add rice and water/broth. Simmer until rice is soft and porridge-like.
  3. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Top with egg, fried garlic, scallions, and a squeeze of calamansi.
Coconut Chia Bowl

A chilled tropical bowl of coconut milk, chia seeds, and fruits — energy-boosting and refreshing.

Coconut Chia Bowl Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp honey or coconut sugar
  • Sliced mango, banana, or papaya
  • Toasted coconut flakes

Instructions:

  1. Mix chia seeds and coconut milk; refrigerate overnight.
  2. Top with fruits and coconut flakes before serving.
Siargao Longganisa

Sweet and garlicky Filipino-style sausage, often served with garlic rice and a sunny-side-up egg, a beloved breakfast treat in the island.

Siargao Longganisa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground pork
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground pepper
  • 1/4 tsp annatto powder (for color, optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 cups garlic rice (sinangag)
  • 2 eggs, fried sunny side up

Instructions:

  1. Mix ground pork with sugar, garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, salt, pepper, and annatto powder.
  2. Form the mixture into small sausage links and refrigerate for 30 minutes to set.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and fry the sausages until golden brown on all sides.
  4. Serve with garlic rice and a fried egg on the side.
Pan de Coco

Soft Filipino bread rolls filled with sweet, grated coconut and sugar. A perfect snack or breakfast treat that embodies the island flavor.

Pan de Coco Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup sugar (for filling)
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, sugar, yeast, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Add warm milk, melted butter, and water. Knead the dough until smooth.
  3. Let the dough rise for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix grated coconut, sugar, and vanilla extract for the filling.
  5. Shape the dough into small rounds and flatten them slightly. Place a spoonful of coconut filling in the center, then fold the dough over the filling to seal.
  6. Coat each roll with breadcrumbs and place on a baking sheet.
  7. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.
  8. Serve warm and enjoy!
Suman

A sweet, sticky rice delicacy cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in banana leaves, often enjoyed as a breakfast or snack.

Suman Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • 1 1/2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2-3 banana leaves (for wrapping)
  • 1 ripe mango, sliced (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the glutinous rice until the water runs clear. In a pot, combine the rice, coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a simmer and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, until the rice has absorbed the coconut milk and is tender.
  2. Once the rice is cooked, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. Cut the banana leaves into square or rectangular pieces. Spoon a portion of the sticky rice onto each banana leaf and fold the leaf around it, forming a small package or cylinder. Secure with toothpicks or string, if necessary.
  3. Steam the wrapped suman for about 30 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and has a smooth, sticky texture.
  4. Serve the suman with slices of ripe mango. Optionally, drizzle some sugar or coconut milk over it for extra sweetness.
